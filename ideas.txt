TO DO:
------

1. REPUTATION SYSTEM
   Two stats: "standing" (legit) and "notoriety" (criminal).
   - Smuggling, bounty hunts, black market jobs increase notoriety
   - Freight, escort, diplomatic jobs increase standing
   - High standing: repair discounts, better crew in hire pool
   - High notoriety: better illegal job pay, but pirate attacks and port fines
   - Show in port header or ship summary
   - Persisted in save file

2. CARGO HOLD MECHANIC
   Utility rooms tagged as cargo bays get capacity slots.
   - Some jobs pay more if you have cargo space
   - Buy goods cheap at one system, sell high at another
   - SYSTEMS already have types (trade hub, mining belt, colony) that imply
     price differences
   - Creates a reason to build cargo rooms beyond adjacency filler

3. CREW MORALE / BONDS
   - Crew who survive dangerous missions together build loyalty (efficiency creep)
   - Crew who never get shore leave slowly lose efficiency
   - Crew on the same ship for many jobs gain a small "veteran" bonus
   - Gives a reason to care about individuals beyond their stat number

4. ROOM UPGRADES
   Spend credits at port to upgrade a room:
   - +25% durability cap for 50% of room's base cost
   - Reduce power draw by 10% for 30% of room's base cost
   - Visual indicator on upgraded rooms (star icon or border)
   - Gives a money sink and reason to invest in existing ship

5. SYSTEM DISCOVERY
   - Start with only nearby systems visible on the job board
   - Longer trips "reveal" new systems
   - Stellar Cartography / Navicomputer utility rooms expand visible range
   - Makes the galaxy feel like it's opening up over time
   - Could tie into a "fog of war" on a galaxy map view

6. DISTRESS CALLS DURING TRAVEL
   Mid-voyage interactive event:
   - Ship receives distress signal, travel pauses
   - Choose: investigate (risk damage, chance of loot/crew/reputation) or ignore
   - Ignoring could have a small morale hit
   - Investigating could lead to mini-encounters (derelict ship, ambush, survivors)
   - Adds player agency during the otherwise passive travel phase

7. CREW PROMOTION SYSTEM
   After X completed jobs, a crew member can be promoted:
   - Engineer → Chief Engineer (reduces wear on ALL engine rooms)
   - Officer → Commander (bonus payout on ALL jobs, not just adjacent)
   - Security → Veteran (damage reduction applies ship-wide)
   - Specialist → Expert (adjacent bonus doubled)
   - Technician → Master Tech (power rooms generate 10% more)
   - Creates attachment to long-serving crew, makes losses hurt

8. GALAXY MAP VIEW
   A 2D top-down or isometric view of the star systems:
   - Shows discovered systems, current location, distances
   - Could be accessible from the main ship builder screen
   - Visual reference for planning routes and jobs
   - Systems color-coded by type (trade hub, hazard, colony, etc.)

9. SHIP LOG / JOURNAL
   Persistent log of all voyages:
   - Destinations visited, credits earned, crew lost
   - Notable events (first time at a system, biggest payday, etc.)
   - Accessible from main screen as a "Captain's Log" tab
   - Adds narrative weight to the journey

10. INSURANCE SYSTEM
    Buy insurance at port for rooms or crew:
    - Room insurance: if durability hits 0, auto-repair to 25% (one-time)
    - Crew insurance: if crew member is lost, receive compensation credits
    - Premium scales with room cost / crew efficiency
    - Adds risk management decisions

11. CONTRACTS / MULTI-LEG JOBS
    Jobs that span multiple systems:
    - "Deliver cargo from Sol → Vega Prime → Elysium" (3-leg route)
    - Higher total payout but must complete all legs
    - Failure on any leg forfeits remaining pay
    - Creates longer-term planning and commitment

12. FACTION SYSTEM
    Different factions control different systems:
    - Federation, Empire, Independents, Pirates, etc.
    - Doing jobs for one faction improves relations, may anger rivals
    - Faction standing unlocks unique rooms or crew at their ports
    - Ties into reputation system naturally
